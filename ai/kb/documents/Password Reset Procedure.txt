# Section 1: Overview
Password resets restore access after expiration, lockout, or suspected compromise. A reset must verify the user's identity and must never disclose an existing password. Support staff will not ask users to reveal passwords or MFA codes.

# Section 2: Steps
Use the approved self-service password reset portal. Enter the SPS account identifier, complete identity verification, and create a unique password that meets the displayed policy. Sign out of other sessions and update saved credentials on managed devices.

# Section 3: Common Issues
Accounts can relock when an old password remains saved in email clients, mobile devices, scheduled tasks, or mapped services. Update or remove stale credentials. If self-service verification fails, use the Service Desk identity-verification process.

# Section 4: When to Contact Support
Contact the Service Desk when self-service reset is unavailable, the account remains locked, or identity verification cannot complete. Contact Security Operations immediately if the reset was not requested or compromise is suspected.
